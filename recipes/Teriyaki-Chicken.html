Teriyaki Chicken

Description

This recipe is meant to mimic the teritaki flavor you might find at a Panda Express. It's good but it also uses more sugar than the average European eats in a day (that's a guess). Eating the chicken, when properly marinated, without the sauce is a perfectly enjoyable meal too! Do what you want, eat the chicken raw if you feel so inclined. This goes well with rice or steam vegetables, probably some other things to but I'm not a chef."

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (boneless and skinless)
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame seed oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Teriyaki Sauce

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp ginger powder
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 1 tbsp cornstartch
  • 2 tbsp water (for the thickening agent)

Steps

Chicken

  1. Add soy suace, sesame seed oil, garlic powerder, and chicken into a plastic bag to mix together
  2. Marinate over night
  3. Add 2 tbsp of oil to a pan over medium heat
  4. When oil is hot, cook chicken (usually 5 minutes on either side)
  5. Slice the chicken into smaller pieces
  6. Chicken is now ready to be served with or without the sauce

Teriyaki Sauce

  1. In a bowl add soy sauce, sugar, garlic powder, ginger powder, lemon juice, and 1/3 cups of water
  2. Mix well
  3. Add mixture to a pan an d set to medium heat, bring to a boil and stir
  4. In a seperate bowl mix the cornstart with the 2 tbsps of water
  5. Add this mixture to the sauce in the pan
  6. Stir, when sauce thickens, it is ready to be served
